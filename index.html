News, Hans Haacke 1969/2008/2018
One of the first electronic artworks to feature a live feed in the gallery, Haackeâ€™s News (1969) consists of a real time printed newsfeed. A pile of paper grows as it spills from the printer on the table onto the floor, and visitors are welcome to pick up portions to read. News does not analyze news content but simply delivers it, spewing out stories upon stories.

News was acquired in 2008 by SFMOMA with a new version of software. As teletype systems and the networks that inform them no longer exists, the work was adapted to print on a dot matix printer via really simple syndication(RSS) feed.

News was to be re-ehxbited at SFMOMA in 2018 fro the Nothing Stable under Heaven show. I was asked to re-write the software as the o 2008 version had become obsolete. In this image, a part of the 2018 sofware is shown. In the rewrite we worked closely with the artists studio, which provided a set of parameters Artist parameters, for example: b) Any printer that produces the news bulletins on paper as they come in, dot matrix or other. In case such paper print-outs can technically no longer be produced in the future, an LED or a comparable presentation could be considered.


Susan Kare and her Macintosh Icons. Susan Kare is an artist and graphic designer best known for her interface elements and typeface contributions to the first Apple Macintosh in the 1980s. The Happy Mac and the Garbage Can

In 2016 along with her design sketchbooks she gifted a collection of 300 floppy disks to SFMOMA and MoMA


In getting the digital files of of these there were many challenges. The 3 1/4 floppy disks that they were on were obsolete. The file systems on the floppies could not be read by modern computers. And the file formats that the images were saved in had become obsolete long ago. We used the Kryoflux hadware which is a floppy disk controller for moderm computers. This come from the software preservation society.

We made extensive use of emulators to access the file systems and formats. Access for curator was a challenge so we we set up a web-based emulator to they could review the icons in their original operating system from a webpage


At a certain point we found color images which was exciting as 8 bit color was new at the time.
